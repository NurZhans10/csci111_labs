Kyzylorda overview

Kyzylorda (also spelled Kzyl-Orda) is a city and administrative center of Kyzylorda oblast (region) of Kazakhstan standing on Syr River.
Kyzylorda population is about 201,000 (2009).

Kyzylorda history

The fortress of Ak-Mechet was founded in 1820 by Omar-khan, the governor of Kokandskoye khanate, as an advanced north-eastern post of the khanate. The fortress was built by over 10,000 Kazakhs from nearby villages. It was founded at the main point of caravan way from Bukhara and Khiva to Central and Northern Kazakhstan and from East to West.
On October 30th, 1917, Soviet power was established in Perovsk. In 1922 the town of Perovsk was renamed into Akmechet. In 1925 it was renamed into Kzyl-Orda, the capital of Kazakh autonomous republic was transferred there from Orenburg, in 1927 it was transferred to Alma-Ata.
